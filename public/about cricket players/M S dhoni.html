M. S. Dhoni, or Mahendra Singh Dhoni, is a former Indian international cricketer and one of the most iconic figures in Indian cricket history. Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, India, Dhoni is renowned for his exceptional leadership skills, calm demeanor under pressure, and his proficiency as a wicketkeeper-batsman.

He made his international debut in 2004 and quickly rose to prominence with his aggressive batting style and sharp wicketkeeping skills. Dhoni is best known for his captaincy tenure, during which he led the Indian cricket team to numerous significant victories, including the ICC T20 World Cup in 2007, the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011, and the ICC Champions Trophy in 2013.

Nicknamed "Captain Cool" for his composed demeanor on the field, Dhoni became one of the most successful captains in Indian cricket history. His unconventional decisions and ability to remain calm in high-pressure situations earned him widespread admiration.

Dhoni retired from Test cricket in 2014, stepping down as captain of the limited-overs formats in 2017. He bid farewell to international cricket entirely in August 2020. Despite retiring from international cricket, Dhoni's influence on the game remains significant, and he continues to be involved in cricket through various roles, including captaining the Chennai Super Kings in the Indian Premier League (IPL).
