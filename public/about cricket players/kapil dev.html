Kapil Dev is a former Indian cricketer, widely regarded as one of the greatest all-rounders in the history of the game. Born on January 6, 1959, in Chandigarh, India, he made his debut for the Indian cricket team in 1978 and went on to have a distinguished career spanning over 16 years.

He is best known for leading the Indian cricket team to its first-ever Cricket World Cup victory in 1983. His iconic innings of 175 runs against Zimbabwe in the same tournament is still remembered as one of the greatest displays of batting in cricket history.

As a fast bowler, Kapil Dev was renowned for his ability to swing the ball both ways and for his aggressive style of play. He was also a dynamic batsman, capable of scoring quick runs in the middle order.

Kapil Dev held the world record for the most Test wickets (434) at the time of his retirement in 1994, a record that stood for almost six years. He was awarded the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award, in recognition of his contributions to Indian cricket.

Post-retirement, he has been involved in various roles in cricket administration and commentary. Kapil Dev remains an iconic figure in Indian cricket history, revered for his leadership, skill, and contribution to the sport.
