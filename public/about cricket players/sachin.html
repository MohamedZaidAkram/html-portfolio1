Sachin Tendulkar, often referred to as the "God of Cricket," is a former Indian cricketer and one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the game. Born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India, he made his international debut for the Indian cricket team at the age of 16. Over his illustrious career spanning 24 years, Tendulkar amassed numerous records, including being the highest run-scorer in both Test and One Day International (ODI) cricket. He is the only player to have scored 100 international centuries, a feat that earned him worldwide acclaim. Tendulkar's elegant strokeplay, impeccable technique, and insatiable hunger for runs made him a cricketing icon. He played a pivotal role in many memorable victories for India and was a source of inspiration for aspiring cricketers around the globe.

Among his countless achievements, Tendulkar was part of the Indian team that won the Cricket World Cup in 2011, fulfilling a dream he had cherished throughout his career. After retiring from international cricket in 2013, Tendulkar continues to be involved in the sport through various roles, including mentoring young players and serving as a cricket commentator. He remains a beloved figure in India and is widely regarded as one of the greatest sportsmen of all time.
